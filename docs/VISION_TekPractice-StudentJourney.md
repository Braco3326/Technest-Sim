# TekPractice — The Student Journey (Higher-Dimension Vision Test)
_Testing the vision from the inside: one real student, end to end. For each beat — **Student lens** (what she feels/does/learns), **Creator lens** (the build decision that makes it work), and **Why** (smart, logical, humane). Companion to VISION_TekPractice-Game.md._

**Meet Nadia.** 19, first year BTS Métiers du son in Paris. Good ear, loves music. Terrified of the physics (E3/PTES) and of the practical exam (E4) because "you either wire it right in front of the jury or you don't." She's tried YouTube and PDFs; nothing stuck. She has 20 minutes tonight and a lot of doubt. Everything below is designed around *her*, not around features.

---

## Beat 1 — The first 5 minutes (Discovery → Day 0)
**Student lens.** She lands on a calm, white page — no flashing, no "PASS 100% GUARANTEED". It asks two things: her exam date and where she feels weak. Then it drops her straight into a *tiny* live scene: "connecte ce micro à la console." She drags a cable, it snaps green, a soft chime, a one-line "voilà — tu viens de faire une prise de son propre." She did something real in 90 seconds. The dread loosens.

**Creator lens.** Onboarding = a 2-question self-assessment that seeds the readiness radar + a **guided-sandbox taste** (palette of 3 items, one obvious goal). No signup wall before the first win. The white aesthetic is a deliberate *threat-reduction* device.

**Why.** First-session success predicts retention more than any feature. Ed-psych calls it *self-efficacy* — "I can do this." We buy it in 90 seconds, before asking for anything. The calm design lowers the cognitive/emotional load that makes anxious learners bounce.

## Beat 2 — The daily loop forms (Day 1–7)
**Student lens.** She opens it the next evening. The dashboard doesn't show 40 options — it shows **one**: "Aujourd'hui : l'alimentation fantôme (5 min)." A micro-lesson, a two-question check, then a level where she forgets the +48V and the condenser stays silent — a gentle toast explains *why*, not just *wrong*. She fixes it, wins, sees a small streak flame light up, and a "Conseil d'Oscar" appears: "le fantôme, c'est le piège classique — je l'ai oublié en TP la première fois aussi." She laughs. She's not alone.

**Creator lens.** A **"single next best action" recommender** (fed by the self-assessment + mistake log) kills decision paralysis. Lessons are short, retrieval-based, spaced. Every rule error (R1–R8) is logged with type. Oscar's tips are **context-triggered** — the phantom tip fires exactly when she meets phantom, never as a wall of text.

**Why.** Habits form from *tiny, frictionless, rewarding* loops (5 min > 50 min for consistency). "One next action" respects that beginners can't self-prioritise. Contextual mentorship builds trust and normalises mistakes — the antidote to exam shame. This is the psychology dimension doing real work.

## Beat 3 — Mastery, and the sandbox opens (Weeks 2–6)
**Student lens.** Her radar for "prise de son" turns from red to amber to green. When she hits a mastery threshold, the app unlocks the **full sandbox**: shelves of real gear by category, and a choice of *rooms* — studio, radio, plateau TV, théâtre, open-air. She picks the théâtre "just to try", grabs an SM58, a stagebox, a console, wires a whole PA, and it *reacts*. Nobody told her to; she wanted to. She saves the rig, names it, shares it. She's no longer revising — she's *making*.

**Creator lens.** **Mastery gates** open freedom gradually (the day-1-overwhelm fix). The sandbox is a **level with no requiredChain** — free graph, *same rules engine, same analytics* — so play still produces a learning signal. Environments are **cheap scene presets** (backdrop + brief + gear subset), so "without limits" stays fast.

**Why.** Autonomy + creativity = intrinsic motivation (self-determination theory). The trick — and the reason this is *never-done-before yet shippable* — is that the sandbox **is assessment in disguise**: her free experiments feed the same readiness model as the tests. Play and study stop being different things.

## Beat 4 — The hard weeks (motivation dip, exam stress)
**Student lens.** Week 5. She's tired, misses two days, expects the app to guilt-trip her. Instead the streak is *forgiven* ("la régularité compte, pas la perfection"), and it quietly serves an easier win to rebuild momentum. A longer "Conseil d'Oscar" appears on a rough evening: "à un mois de l'exam j'étais persuadé d'échouer — voilà ce qui m'a aidé à tenir." She keeps going.

**Creator lens.** **Adaptive difficulty** + **streak-forgiveness** + a **tone engine** that never punishes. Mentor content is *timed to emotional low points* (detected via drop in activity / rising error rate), not spammed. Anti-burnout is a feature, not an afterthought.

**Why.** Most study tools lose people exactly here, through shame-based gamification (broken streaks, red numbers). We design for the *dip* on purpose: forgiveness sustains long-term behaviour better than pressure. Oscar's authentic "I nearly quit too" is the single most powerful retention asset — and impossible for a competitor or a raw AI to fake.

## Beat 5 — The final stretch (exam simulation)
**Student lens.** Three weeks out. She switches to **Tests** — timed, exam-format, no hints, scored like the real épreuves. First score stings, but the report is precise: "solide en câblage, fragile en audionumérique (échantillonnage, wordclock)." The app builds her a **last-mile plan** targeting exactly those gaps. Her radar fills. For the first time she can *see* she's ready — not hope, *see*.

**Creator lens.** **Exam-fidelity test mode** (format, timing, no scaffolding) mapped to **E3/E4/E51 and BC01–BC04**. A **readiness score** derived from real performance, plus a **weak-spot recommender** for the last mile. The mistake-history trends toward zero (Oscar's own success metric).

**Why.** Calibrated confidence beats blind confidence *and* blind fear. Retrieval practice under exam conditions is the most evidence-backed transfer technique there is. Showing readiness as data converts anxiety into a plan — the psychology and pedagogy dimensions fusing.

## Beat 6 — Exam day & after (transfer, and the loop closes)
**Student lens.** In the practical, the jury asks her to wire a chain. Her hands already know it — she's done it 100 times, in five different rooms. The theory questions are muscle memory. She passes. Weeks later she gets a nudge: "partage ta config préférée / laisse un conseil aux prochains." She becomes a voice for the next Nadia.

**Creator lens.** The whole design optimised for **transfer** (real gear, real rooms, spaced retrieval, varied contexts). Post-success, students become **community + mentor content** — the loop closes: successful users seed the trust that recruits the next cohort.

**Why.** The product's real success metric isn't engagement — it's *she passed and it felt earned*. And a graduate who credits you becomes your most credible marketing. Business, pedagogy, and community align on the same outcome.

---

## Creator's cross-cutting laws (the "why" behind everything)
1. **Every session must produce a win and a signal.** A win for her motivation, a signal for the readiness model.
2. **One next action, always.** Beginners can't prioritise; the app does it.
3. **Play = assessment.** The sandbox feeds the same analytics as tests, or it's just a toy.
4. **Freedom follows mastery.** Gate the sandbox palette so day-1 isn't overwhelming, but always give a taste.
5. **Design for the dip.** Forgiveness and adaptive ease at low points; never shame.
6. **The mentor voice is a feature, not decoration.** Context-timed, authentic, irreplaceable.
7. **Readiness, never vanity.** Progress = mapped to the real référentiel, not XP.
8. **White calm = lower anxiety = more learning.** The aesthetic is pedagogy.
9. **Content is data; environments are presets.** "Without limits" stays fast because nothing new needs new engine code.

## Honest failure modes (test from the creator side)
- **Sandbox becomes a toy** → fix: it must write to the readiness model, or cut it.
- **Overwhelm on day one** → fix: guided sandbox + one-next-action + mastery gates.
- **Shame-gamification creeps in** → fix: forgiveness, no punishing leaderboards.
- **Content correctness drifts** → fix: *you*, the graduate, are the review gate; nothing ships unverified.
- **Perf dies with big rigs** → fix: budgets, LOD, device caps, 2D fallback for weak laptops.
- **Free tier gives away the value** → fix: free = one room + a few levels; subscription = full sandbox + tests + analytics.

## Bottom line
Tested from the inside, the vision holds — and it's strongest exactly where competitors are absent: it turns *revision* into *making*, *anxiety* into *a readiness plan*, and a *lonely grind* into a *mentored journey*. The engineering stays fast because the ambition ("without limits") is expressed as combinatorial freedom over a real, bounded system, and the pedagogy stays honest because play and assessment are the same loop. Build it beat by beat, in this order, and each release already helps a real student.
